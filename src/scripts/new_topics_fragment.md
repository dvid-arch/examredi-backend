
## Computer Studies
### 1. Fundamentals
*   **Computer Basics:** Hardware, software, input/output devices, storage, CPU, end users, data processing cycle, peripheral devices.
*   **Information Processing:** Data, Information, processing steps, collating, analysis.

### 2. Applications and Systems
*   **Software:** Operating systems (Windows, Linux), application software, word processing, spreadsheets, database management, SDLC (System Development Life Cycle), presentation software.
*   **Programming:** Algorithms, flowcharts, languages (BASIC, C, Java, Python), assembler, compiler, interpreter, source code, object code, loop, logic gates.
*   **Security and Ethics:** Viruses, antivirus, cracking, hacking, ethics, privacy, copyright, cybercrime.

## Physical and Health Education (PHE)
### 1. Sports and Games
*   **Athletics:** Track and field, sprints, middle distance, long distance, relays, hurdles, jumps (long, high, triple), throws (shot put, discus, javelin), starting blocks.
*   **Ball Games:** Soccer, football, basketball, volleyball, handball, hockey, tennis, racket, net, goal, pass, dribble, smash, serve.
*   **Gymnastics:** Floor activities, apparatus, tumbling, vaulting, balance beam.

*   **Human Anatomy:** Skeletal system, muscular system, respiratory, circulatory, digestive, nervous systems, posture.
*   **Health:** Personal hygiene, nutrition, first aid, safety, diseases (communicable/non-communicable), drug abuse, alcohol, tobacco.

## History
### 1. Nigerian History
*   **Pre-Colonial:** Kingdoms and Empires (Oyo, Benin, Kanem-Borno, Hausa States, Igala, Jukun), political systems, trade, Islam, Christianity.
*   **Colonial:** British conquest, amalgamation, indirect rule, nationalist movements, constitutional development, resistance.
*   **Post-Independence:** First Republic, military rule, civil war, Second Republic, Third/Fourth Republics, coups.

### 2. African and World History
*   **Africa:** Scramble for Africa, partition, resistance, independence movements, OAU/AU, ECOWAS, apartheid.
*   **World:** World Wars, Cold War, United Nations, League of Nations.

## Islamic Religious Knowledge (IRK)
### 1. The Quran and Hadith
*   **Quran:** Revelation, compilation, themes, surahs (Al-Fatiha, Al-Ikhlas, etc.), Tafsir, Makkan/Madinan surahs.
*   **Hadith:** Collections (Bukhari, Muslim), sunnah, transmission (isnad, matn), authenticity.

### 2. Tawhid and Fiqh
*   **Articles of Faith:** Belief in Allah, Angels, Books, Prophets, Day of Judgment, Destiny (Qadar).
*   **Pillars of Islam:** Kalima/Shahada, Salat (prayer), Zakat (alms), Sawm (fasting), Hajj (pilgrimage).
*   **Fiqh:** Sharia sources, family law (marriage, divorce, inheritance), Taharah (purification).

### 3. History
*   **Life of Prophet Muhammad:** Birth, call to prophethood, Hijrah, battles (Badr, Uhud), conquest of Makkah, farewell pilgrimage.
*   **Caliphate:** Rightly Guided Caliphs (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali), Umayyads/Abbasids (briefly).

## Music
### 1. Theory and Composition
*   **Rudiments:** Staff, clefs, notes, rests, time signatures, key signatures, intervals, scales (major/minor), triads, chords, cadence, transposition, modulation.
*   **Forms:** Binary, ternary, rondo, sonata, fugue.

### 2. Music in Society
*   **Nigerian/African Music:** Traditional instruments (idiophones, membranophones, chordophones, aerophones), functions of music, Nigerian composers (e.g., Fela Sowande, Akin Euba).
*   **Western Music:** History periods (Baroque, Classical, Romantic, 20th Century), composers (Bach, Mozart, Beethoven).
